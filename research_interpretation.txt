Research Flow Summary:
-----------------------
1. Team-Level Mean Effects:
   - Bootstrapped means and 95% CIs show direction of average improvement.
   - Double-drafting: slightly negative mean, widest dispersion.
   - Single: modest positive mean, moderate variance.
   - Zero: near-zero mean, lowest variance.

2. Volatility Analysis:
   - Levene’s test checks unequal variances (p < 0.05 ⇒ variance differs).
   - Double-drafting = largest standard deviation — 'boom-bust' risk.

3. Weighted & Hierarchical Models:
   - WLS corrects heteroskedasticity; mixed model captures team noise.
   - Mean effects remain small but variance significant.

4. Snap Share Augmented Models:
   - avg_db_snap_share quantifies DB utilization.
   - Positive coefficient ⇒ greater DB participation → stronger improvement.
   - Interaction term shows double-drafting + high usage amplifies variance.

5. Risk–Return Framing:
   - risk_return_draft_strategies.png visualizes:
        x = volatility (risk), y = average improvement (return)
   - Double-drafting: high risk, high potential return.
   - Single: moderate.
   - Zero: low risk, low return.
